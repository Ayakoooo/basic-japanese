Japanese Core Sentence

Japanese sentences are pretty simple. We have a main car and an engine
Two types of sentences exist: A does B | Sakura walks and A is B | Sakura is Japanese

Sakura walks | A does B A Sakura + が B aruku
Sakura is Japanese | A is B A Sakura + が B Nihonjin + だ

Describing words end with い

A Pen + が B akai ∅ だ
Note: No need to use だ, because い

The practice sentence (can be in full Japanese, hiragana or romaji)

If you use romaji you should place a hyphen between a particle and its noun to show that they are part of the same carriage.
歌を歌う or うたをうたう or uta-wo utau

If there is an invisible carriage, please write the sentence a second time showing the zero pronoun and its particle

∅が歌を歌う or ∅がうたをうたう or ∅-ga uta-wo utau

Give the English meaning

Note: since there is no singular/plural distinction in Japanese grammar structure, “(I) sing songs” would also be correct.
